On Lok Multi Cultural Music Therapy Event

by Pranav rameshjune 13th, 2019


A second successful music therapy event was held in On Lok Lifeways, a senior residential community in San Jose. Music therapists Taffy Su and Sylvester Sroka joined us again in providing a spectacular afternoon music therapy session for seniors.
This event was a cultural mixing pot of music which allowed seniors to have a fresh experience. Ms. Su expertly used her knowledge of different cultural music to provide seniors with a unique perspective on music therapy. The percussion and drums are drawn from African roots while the songs themselves were drawn from American, Asian, and Latin American inspirations. A combination of these music types appealed to the varied cultural demographic of the seniors at the event. Moreover, it exposed new exciting forms of music to seniors who might not have otherwise heard them.
The value of multicultural music is centered around the fact that seniors felt more connected than ever to the music they were singing and playing. It aligned with their cultural identity, while also opening the gateway into their memories associated with their culture.
Despite the vast background of cultures, music was the common unifying medium which allowed the seniors to interact with not only ReMind members, but also other fellow seniors. It allowed them to become more connected with themselves as well as the people around them, greatly improving their emotional and mental health. The qualitative benefits of music therapy were present and the ethnographic integration of different cultures greatly compounded its effects.
The ReMind team was able to drum, sing, and co-lead the session with Ms. Su and Mr. Sroka.
